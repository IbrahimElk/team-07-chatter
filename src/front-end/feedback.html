Keystroke Fingerprinting

Keystroke fingerprinting is a technique that uses an individual's typing patterns to identify them based on their typing speed. Each person has a unique typing style, similar to a fingerprint, which can be used to differentiate them from others. When typing, a combination of keystrokes is used, including letters, numbers, and special characters. Keystroke fingerprinting algorithms analyse the timing of these keystrokes to create a unique profile for each user, which can be used to identify the user with a high level of accuracy. The process of keystroke fingerprinting involves collecting data on an individual's typing pattern, specifically the time between different keystrokes, to create a model representing their typing style. Once the model is created, it can be used to compare future typing samples and determine whether they were made by the same individual or an imposter. Keystroke fingerprinting has a broad range of applications, including in cybersecurity and biometrics. Its primary use is for security purposes, to prevent other people from sending messages on your behalf, making it more relevant to biometrics. Despite its effectiveness, keystroke fingerprinting has limitations. Changes in an individual's typing patterns, such as typing speed or keyboard layout, can affect the algorithm's accuracy. Additionally, individuals with physical disabilities that impact their typing patterns may render the technique less reliable. In conclusion, keystroke fingerprinting is a powerful tool for identifying individuals based on their unique typing style.
